About Scott Black

Scott Black has been in service as Northwest Bloomington’s Alderman since being elected in 2013. He began his public service representing Historic Bloomington on the McLean County Board in March of 2010 and served on that body until 2013. Originally from Mt. Prospect, Illinois, Scott moved to Bloomington in 2006 at the start of his college career at Illinois Wesleyan University. He graduated from that institution in 2010 with a degree in Political Science.

As someone with a lifelong passion for local government, Scott enjoys chatting with residents about local issues. These conversations inspired him to host two community forums aimed at engaging community members with their elected officials. In 2011, Scott was selected as one of McLean County’s 25 under 25 group. In 2015, Scott was awarded the McLean County Chamber of Commerce 20 under 40 award.

Scott is employed by Afni, Inc since June of 2010. Currently, he works in the Human Resources Department. On top of his public and professional commitments, Scott actively gives back to the community. He’s been active in the United Way, St. Jude’s Children’s Hospital, Green Top Grocery, West Bloomington Revitalization Project, and Big Brothers Big Sisters organizations.

In his spare time, Scott enjoys biking, traveling and cooking.
